Created 2011-12-11, updated 2017-05-18
  • aws
  • puppet

So I'm "puppetizing" my EC2 servers. The approach I used to use before Puppet was to create a base, EBS-backed AMI and push as much configuration as possible out onto a separate EBS "data" volume. That configuration would get pulled in via include directives (where the service in question supported it) or via creative mounting (eg. setting up /etc/fstab to mount /data/mysql at /var/lib/mysql). Moving towards Puppet means less of that, and more of just sticking the configuration files in Puppet and telling it to put them where they belong on the base AMI.
