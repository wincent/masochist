  1. Greg Hurrell 2011-05-26T02:32:33Z

    What are the contents of your ~/.vimrc?

    Command-T itself shouldn't be able to stop the MacVim window from appearing, as it doesn't actually do anything until you explicitly call it up, for example with a command like :CommandT or a mapping like <Leader>t.

    (In any case, if you miscompile it, which is the most common mistake people make, you should see an error message, not a total failure of the window to appear.)
