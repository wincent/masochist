  1. Greg Hurrell 2010-08-11T09:43:16Z

    Can't reproduce, I'm afraid. Can you provide more information?

    • What do you mean by "crash" exactly?
    • What version of Vim are you using?
    • What version of Ruby?

    Most crashes involving Command-T (in fact, every crash that's ever been reported) have been due to compiling it using a different version of Ruby than the one that Vim was linked against (ie. 32-bit vs 64-bit, or 1.8.x vs 1.9.x etc), so my suspicion is that that's what you are seeing here.
