Command-T on Ubuntu

  1. anonymous 2010-10-29T17:29:41Z

    Is it possible?

    I've just compiled without any troubles, but I can't get the CTRL+T working (I don't have Command button on my laptop...).

  2. Greg Hurrell 2010-10-29T19:20:21Z

    Try <Leader>t (what your <Leader> key is depends on your configuration; it is usually \ by default but , is a frequently-used customization). See the help (:h CommandT) for more info, specifically the mappings section (:h command-t-mappings).
